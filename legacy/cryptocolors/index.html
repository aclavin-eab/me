Crypto Colors is an encryption game. The color swatches appear in random order and your goal is to decrypt the order. Arrange them by their apparent lightness (brightest colors first, darkest last), by clicking on them and moving them to their appropriate location. Watch closely for the blocks to seperate from the encrypted bunch with thin white gaps. They will then lock in place, and you will be one step closer to decrypting the code. This game was created by Andrew Clavin, Brandon Mahone, and Raymond Zapp as a part of the CESI / University of Portsmouth Game Jam Game Jam 2012 Close

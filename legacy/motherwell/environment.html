“Every man becomes, to a certain degree, what the people he generally converses with are.”

Motherwell had many people coming in and out of his life. He married four times times; Maria Emilia Ferreira y Moyeros, Betty Little, Helen Frankenthaller, and Renate Ponsold. He had two daughters, both he was very close with. In the art he was involved with surrealists and dadaists like Max Ernst and Marcel Duchamp as well as modernist sculptors and abstract expressionists like David Smith and Barnet Newman. He was also close friends with Clement Greenberg, the champion critic of the New York School, whom upon Motherwell’s death proclaimed, “although he
is underrated today, in my opinion he was the very best of the Abstract Expressionist painters.”

“For some reason that I do not know, painting-wise I am often affected by places after I leave them”

Robert Motherwell was born in Auburdeen, Washington. His early education was in California, specifically Stanford. Motherwell’s admiration of the wide open Pacific spaces can be seen in his painting. At the age 20, he traveled in Europe extensively, starting in Paris and ending in Scotland. Afterwards he attended Harvard followed by Columbia, where he studied under artist Meyer Schapiro. Motherwell spent a period in Mexico, which permanently added warm colors to his pallette. Most of his later life was spent on the East Coast, eventually passing away in Provincetown, Massachusetts in 1991.

“The surrealists had the laudable aim of bringing the spiritual to everyone.”

Surrealists and the closely related Dada crowd
were the first group of artists Motherwell became socially involved with, including Max Ernst and Marcel Duchamp. Much of his early writing had to
do with Surrealism, as well as his first experiences editing publications such as Dada Painters
and Poets: An Anthology

“I enjoyed David [Smith]’s friendship more completely than any artist I have ever known.”

Motherwell was very close friends with Smith, greatly admiring his openness. David was also close friends with Helen Frankenthaler. When Motherwell married Frankenthaler, he recalls that the three of them became a trio with a special dimension. Motherwell had many other friends in the New York School of Abstract Expressionists including Mark Rothko, Jackson Pollock, and Barnett Newman.

“I loved the Indian market places, and
I loved the Mexican folk art color—magenta, bright lemon yellow, lime green, indigo, vermilion, shocking pink.”

Robert Motherwell traveled to Mexico in 1941 to follow his mentor at the time, Roberto Matta. While there he gained an intense interest in the Mexican Revolution and developed a warm colored scheme in his paintings which became a permanent part of
his painting milieu.

“If you want to drive anyone insane, rear him in an environment without a sensne of limits.”

“If I had absolute control of American museums—God forbid!—my first, emergency act would be to install Charles Egan or Clement Greenberg to choose all the works to be shown at the Whitney Museum of American Art; and my second, long-range act would be to redistribute all the great artists, so that each museum, according to its importance, place, and scale would have a preponderance—preferably all—of the works in American public collections of specific artists.

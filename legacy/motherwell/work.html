“Painting is a medium in which the mind can actualize itself; it is a medium of thought.”

Motherwell was most known as a painter, but had a prolific career as a printmaker, collagist, writer, and editor. Among his paintings, the Elegy to the Spanish series are the most known, cosely followed by the Open series. His visual language largely consisted of archetypes that he identified as subconscious element from his past.

Next

“I have written hundreds of thousands of words during my life, though I loathe the act of writing, its lack of phisicality and sensuality.”

Motherwell was an articulate writer and respected editor. He worked on various publications, notably the Documents of Modern Art and The Dada Painters and Poets: an Anthology. Through writing he earned the title of spokesperson of Abstract Expressionism. Motherwell received grief from dealers, galleries, and others for his extensively articulated thoughts— at times he regretted ever writing a single word.

“Even though I have worked on the Open series for two years now, the series retains a certain mystery for me that I prefer to leave inviolate.”

Motherwell created the Open composition incessently, recalling that the he found the open form clearly in his work even before he purposefully constructed it. It consists of a rectangular form in the canvas, seperating it from the rest of the canvas. He played with this motif again and again, exploring the beauty of its simple form.

“[The Ellegies to the Spanish Republic were] general metaphors of the contrast between life and death and their interrelation.”

One of Motherwell’s most famous painting series
was his Ellegies to the Spanish Republic. This series consisted of black vertical bands and circles. It was visually representative of freedom and restraint, an expression with the Spanish revolution in mind. Over time as the series matured, they became about more universal concepts, no longer only addressing the dead Spanish Republic.

“The sensation of phsyically operating on the world is very strong in the medium of collage…it possesses feeling because all the decisions are made on the grounds of feeling.”

Throughout his career Motherwell often used collage within his paintings and, specifically in his later work, on their own. Working with collage allowed him to evoke a highly hugman feeling in his composition with more ease than a painting.
